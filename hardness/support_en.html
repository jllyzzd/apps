Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Why are some results in parentheses? e.g., (68.5)

This indicates that the converted value is outside the standard defined range for that specific scale. For reference purposes, the App still provides an extrapolated value based on the algorithm. However, for critical engineering applications, please use these values with caution and consult the original standards.

2. What standards is the App based on?

Our core conversion algorithms strictly adhere to the following international standards:

  • ASTM E140: Standard Hardness Conversion Tables for Metals
  • ISO 18265: Metallic materials — Conversion of hardness values

3. How is Tensile Strength calculated?

Tensile Strength (MPa) is estimated based on an empirical formula derived from Vickers Hardness (HV), primarily applicable to non-alloy and low-alloy steels. The approximation is: Tensile Strength (MPa) ≈ HV × 3.3. For other specific materials, please refer to specialized material handbooks.

4. How do I change the language?

Hardness supports multiple languages (English, Chinese, German, Japanese, etc.). The App defaults to your system language. You can change the language specifically for Hardness in your device's system "Settings".
